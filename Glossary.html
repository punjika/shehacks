Choose language

Savings Account

A savings account is a type of bank account where you can deposit money and earn interest on your savings.

Investing

Putting money towards assets that grow over time, like stocks, bonds, or real estate.

Compound Interest

Interest earned on both the initial principal amount and the accumulated interest over time.

Debt

Money you owe to a lender, like a credit card company or bank loan.

Interest Rate

The percentage charged on borrowed money (the higher the rate, the more you pay in interest).

Credit Score

A number that reflects your creditworthiness, impacting your ability to borrow money and the interest rate you'll be offered.

Debit Card

A card linked to your checking account used for direct payments.

Credit Card

A card that allows you to borrow money up to a credit limit, with interest charged on unpaid balances.

Emergency Fund

Money saved for unexpected expenses, like car repairs or medical bills.

Retirement Savings

Money saved for your retirement years.

Financial Goal

A specific target you aim to achieve with your money, like buying a house or saving for a vacation.

Stock

Ownership shares in a company, representing a portion of its ownership and potential for profit.

Bond

A loan you make to a company or government, earning interest in return.

Mutual Fund

A professionally managed investment pool that combines money from many investors to buy various assets.

Asset

Anything you own with monetary value, like cash, investments, or property.

Liability

A financial obligation you owe, like debt or bills.

Net Worth

The difference between your assets and liabilities, representing your overall financial health.
